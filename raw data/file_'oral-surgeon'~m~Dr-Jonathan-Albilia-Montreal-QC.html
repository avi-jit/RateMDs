When I was 12 years old (I am now 19), my orthodontist referred me to Dr Albilia who diagnosed me with mandibular hyperplasia. Dr Albilia recommended we wait until I was older before doing anything since I was not done growing yet and my parents agreed with this decision. 3 years later, at the age of 15, we went to see Dr. Albilia once more and decided to go through with a surgery that would restructure my jaw and essentially resolve the problem. My mother was hesitant of the risks that this surgery could pose but he explained to us the risk in details and the very low chance of these risk occurring. Additionally in order to go through with the surgery, Dr. Albilia had to remove all 4 of my wisdom teeth and he did a wonderful job doing so. Finally, in June 2013, I had the surgery I had been waiting for for years. Along with the help of another surgeon, Dr. Albilia reconstructed my entire lower jaw. After the surgery, Dr Albilia was very kind and very helpful in terms of my recovery. However, a year after the surgery, my scar still was still very red and swollen. He told us that he was going to try and re-scar it which might resolve the problems. We went ahead with the re-scarring process and I was to see him again for follow ups every six months, which never ended up happening and I haven't followed up with him since. To this day, my scar has not healed properly and after consulting with a dermatologist, I discovered that the type of scar that I had was a keloid scar and that the re-scarring process that Dr Albilia thought would help is actually something that should never be done with a keloid since it guarantees making it worse. I now find myself spending a lot of money to fix this scar with injections and laser. Overall, Dr Albilia was a great surgeon during the time that I was going through the steps before the surgery and during the surgery but he sort of fell short after the he tried to fix my scar. However, I would still recommend him to anybody who is going through what I went through back then.
