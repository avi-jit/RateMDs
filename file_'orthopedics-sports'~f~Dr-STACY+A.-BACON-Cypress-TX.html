I suffered a spinal cord injury at age 15 that left me paralyzed from the neck down. I did eventually regained the ability to walk again, but some residual paralysis and a great amount of muscle spasticity remained. After 40 years of walking with my disability and increased spasicity my foot had now permanently turned in and pointed down. It was now an extreme struggle to walk unassisted and without tripping. I began trying many therapies and Botox treatments. None had any success to reduce the spasticity . I asked my primary doctor if he though any type of surgery could be done to correct this. He said this would likely be a very specialized procedure and be done in the Medical Center downtown. By chance, another doctor for an unrelated item suggested I see Dr. Bacon for her opinion about my foot. The first time I went to see Dr. Bacon, I did spend some extra time in the waiting room. ( All of my follow up visits were right on schedule ) When I met Dr. Bacon, she examined my foot and we discussed the issues I was having. She very quickly, clearly and decisively suggested a surgical procedure that would reverse the problem I was having. I had the procedure the following week. Yes, I definitely had some post-op pain. Yes, I wanted something stronger for pain , but I've never met anyone that didn't . I didn't ask . It was a few days, I made it. I will have to say, the first time I saw my foot I was in shock. I truly realized this was quiet a procedure. I thought my foot would never look the same, but today I have hardly a trace of scaring. Unbelievable ! After several more weeks in a cast and walking cast it was time for the big reveal. I put on a regular shoe and walked in the hallway in the office. It was a very emotional moment . I was walking flat on my foot with my toes pointing forward. The first time my mother saw me, she cried. For me and my family, Dr. Bacon had performed a miracle. I am currently doing some therapy and completing the healing process. My procedure turned out better than I expected. My opinion of Dr. Bacon is as follows; At first, Dr. Bacon wasn't a person of many extra words. The first couple of visits were all business, quick and done. She is busy. My primary doctor is the same way, but I wouldn't go any where else. I want a good doctor, not a good conversationalist. Now when I see Dr. Bacon, the visits are still quick but we always manage to get a few laughs in . I feel Dr. Bacon always kept me informed about the treatment I was getting and always answered any questions I had. I didn't feel left out. Her staff were all very friendly and helpful. My family and I love Dr. Bacon. She gave me a better life. I would certainly recommend her to anyone.
