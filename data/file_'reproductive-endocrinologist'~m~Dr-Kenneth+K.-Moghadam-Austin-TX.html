Having been to multiple fertility doctors, I can say that Dr. Moghadam and his team truly provide exceptional service. We had no difficulties conceiving our first child, born when I was 36, but were unable to have another on our own after years of trying. Our first fertility doctor recommended IUI--we tried 6 cycles with no luck. After ceasing treatment I got pregnant at 41, but then miscarried at 6 weeks. Later, we tried a larger fertility clinic--the doctor was ready to start IVF but we did not move forward with the treatment, as we felt rushed and were not prepared to begin IVF. I again got pregnant, but it was biochemical and did not progress long at all. Afterwards, we decided to start Iva Keene's fertility coaching program, and made some major dietary changes like cutting out wheat and dairy, as well as beginning her recommended supplements. By this point I had read Dr. Moghadam's favorable reviews, and we decided to give things one last try, certain that we would need to take out loans for IVF. Dr. Moghadam listened to our history and surprised us when he said he did not think IVF would be the best course of treatment for us, as we had managed to get pregnant on our own. He suspected that at my age (42) the issue was egg quality, and recommended Follistim to stimulate multiple eggs in the ovulatory cycle, to increase the odds of a viable pregnancy. We did not start the treatment at the next possible cycle, as we were dealing with the loss of a family member, but when I returned to follow up he did not pressure us at all. We ordered the fertility drugs and while I responded well to the Follistim, we had a negative pregnancy test on our first try. Even though I had dealt with years of not being able to conceive and thought myself prepared to handle the disappointment, I was moved by the personal call from Dr. Moghadam, offering a compassionate and sensitive ear as he talked us through the next step. The next cycle I had ovarian cysts (a complication from the previous month's treatment) and so could not use the medicine. The next month--success! We had a positive pregnancy test, with my HCG rising to promising levels. We "graduated"from Dr. Moghdam's office after the Panorama test showed a low-risk pregnancy. Why did we like Dr. Moghadam so much? For starters, he recommended a different course of treatment from previous doctors, and he got us results. But there were so many other reasons--we loved the feel of the office from our initial visit, from the soothing waiting room to the comfortable ambiance of a smaller practice, this office felt different, and we felt we were truly getting personalized care from him and his amazing staff. When my job presented schedule complications, they always worked with my schedule to get me in. (Some have commented on wait times--a few times I did have to wait, but considering the level of care he gives his patients the occasional wait is well worth it.) And as I said earlier, Dr. Moghadam was never high-pressure with us. He listened to my concerns and was always respectful--for instance, while he did not think the supplements I was taking would have a big factor on fertility, he respected that I wanted to take them (as he of course did not deem them harmful). As evidence of his dedication, he gives patients his personal line for after-hours questions, and was helpful and reassuring the few times I needed to contact him outside of business hours. Overall, I just felt so cared for and supported by him and his fabulous staff on all visits. I've truly never experienced that level of care from any other provider; we are certainly grateful to have found them, and as I am almost 34 weeks, we are thrilled to soon meet our sweet baby girl.

Biography

Dr. Moghadam is the Medical Director and founder of the Austin Fertility Institute. He is an experienced reproductive medicine specialist, with board certification in both obstetrics/gynecology and reproductive endocrinology/infertility. Dr. Moghadam grew up in the Chicago area, and obtained his undergraduate degree from Illinois Wesleyan University in Bloomington, IL. Upon graduation from the Tulane University School of Medicine in New Orleans, LA, he returned to the Midwest, completing his residency in obstetrics and gynecology at Indiana University. He then obtained accredited subspecialty fellowship training in reproductive endocrinology and infertility at the University of Cincinnati. Despite a diversity of activities and interests, Dr. Moghadam derives his greatest satisfaction from applying his background and experience to the compassionate care of his patients. He is an avid sports enthusiast, tennis player, and enjoys the Austin music scene. He and his wife Tammy have been married since 1995 and have four sons.
