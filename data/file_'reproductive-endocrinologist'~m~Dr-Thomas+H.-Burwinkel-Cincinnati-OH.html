Even though I have not yet successfully carried to term under Dr. Burwinkel's care, I've been a patient long enough to feel confident in rating the quality of care I've received. Firstly, his staff - nurses Tara and Maria at the Rockwood office have some of the gentlest blood draws I've ever seen and they genuinely seem to care about what's going on in my treatments even if it's unrelated to what they're doing at the time or I've seen a different nurse that day - they'll still stop for a few minutes to ask how I'm doing. I dont feel like "just another chart" when I talk with them. The new receptionist at the Rockwood office (I haven't caught her name yet) is so wonderfully efficient - I walk in and she has me checked in before I even reach the desk. A relief on some of the days I'm so flustered and emotional that I can barely remember my own name. Rarely is Dr. B late for an appointment - like most doctors, there can be unanticipated emergencies throughout the day that throw off the scheduling, but he's typically on time (if not early). Regarding actual treatment, I had one chemical pregnancy and one miscarriage while under Dr. B's care, along with a current pregnancy outcome TBD. Throughout each step of treatment he has been able to provide sound reasoning for each treatment option every step of the way. Each suggestion has been backed up by research or experience and he has never gone gung-ho into treatment without careful consideration of risks vs benefits. He has never sugar coated my situation or the risks of certain types of treatment, but has always been kind in discussing what these mean for my future fertility options. As for his manner and approach, it might not be for everyone. However, he's shown nothing but empathy and compassion - in some cases, he's been more therapeutic to talk to about my fertility situation than my actual therapist!
