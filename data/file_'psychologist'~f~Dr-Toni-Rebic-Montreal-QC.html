Short version: Toni is amazing and one of the best things to ever happen to me. Long version: II've been to many therapists since I was 31 and admitted I needed help, and they all helped me in some way, mostly by giving me insight into why I didn't understand my own feelings and behavior. After listening to my story in full (taking notes on her phone, which at first was offputting -- was she playing Angry Birds? -- but then I got used to it, because she proved later she was definitely paying close attention), she said something to me that made a huge difference: "You've got a lot of insight. That's good. But you don't need any more insight. You need better habits to turn those insights into good feelings, and you need to practice those habits. Luckily, that's exactly what I do." And she does. I feel like I've made much more progress in the last three months seeing Toni (and only once every three weeks, the rest of the time I practice the techniques she gives me) and going to her workshops than any other similar period of my life. Her prices are reasonable for a therapist, but on the high end of that spectrum -- but that's okay, with her technique, you don't need to see her as often or as long, she gives you the tools to do most of it yourself. The workshops, in groups, are lower priced, and there's one a month on Gratitude for $10. I'm a scientist, so I was kind of resistant to the idea of "vibrations" and "being in alignment" and her occasional misquoting of laws of physics when making an analogy, but that's okay, these are metaphors and they. just. work. I'm actually meditating every day, and the me of six months ago would spit out his coffee in laughter and shock to know that, but it really helps. I did not really know what meditation was or how it could help me, but Toni showed me the way. I still have a long way to go (I'm coming into this with some serious mental problems like PTSD and substance abuse), but for the first time in a long time I have hope. And unlike with some therapists where I felt "cured" after a while and then slid quickly back into self-defeating behavior, I know understand that, as Toni says, learning to feel good is like learning a new languauge -- you learn a bunch of new words all at once, and that's exciting, but you realize in order to actually speak it you need to practice it, every day (or almost), or you forget it quickly. With Toni's help I'm learning the language of learning to feel good about myself, and doing a realistic process every day to work towards that goal. Really, I cannot recommend Toni enough. In three months, I feel the effects of what I've learned from her every day, and they help me, a lot, every day. Yay Toni!
