I am a 51 year old man who has been battling glaucoma since early 20's. I have already suffered significant vision loss and have continuously searched for options to limit future loss. After a trabeculectomy 4 years ago caused immediate incremental loss of vision followed by closure which mitigated effectiveness of pressure control, I was "done" with procedures for fear of acceleration of the very loss of vision I was targeting to decelerate. Other Ophthalmologists I saw for options talked "at" me and told me what I "had to do" and were irritable when I asked "why" and "at what risk". Dr. Moster was referred to me by a trusted Ophthalmologist and my first visit was 2 years ago. The first visit outlined options but in a manner that was consultative. The second visit, almost a year later, she presented the option of canaloplasty. In the last 6 months I have had canaloplasty on both eyes, with a resulting pressure in both of 13 from 18-20. As a patient, I would recommend Dr. Moster not only for her surgical skills and experience with up to date glaucoma treatment, but also her (and her team) for creating an open, honest dialogue on options. I do think it is worthwhile to point out that in the many visits I drive to Philly to see her, every time she was educating, training, and explaining to residents and staff members. On a personal level I take this as a sign of not only her passion for “getting it right” but in the investment to teach and train others. We will see if the canaloplasty is short or long term, but I would recommend Dr. Moster and her team for top quality glaucoma care and treatment.

Biography

Marlene Moster, MD is a Professor of Ophthalmology at Jefferson Medicine College in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. She is an attending surgeon at Wills Eye Hospital and also holds appointments at Thomas Jefferson University Hospital, Temple University School of Medicine, Lankenau Hospital, and Riddle Memorial Hospital. Dr. Moster graduated from SUNY Health Science Center at Syracuse, New York in 1979, underwent a postgraduate ophthalmology residency at the University of Pittsburgh Eye and Ear Hospital, and was subsequently accepted to the Glaucoma Fellowship at the Wills Eye Hospital, Philadelphia. She is board certified in ophthalmology and licensed in Pennsylvania, New Jersey, Delaware, and Florida. Dr. Moster has authored and co-authored over 60 peer-reviewed articles in various ophthalmologic journals. She also plays an important role in the evaluation of research material as a manuscript referee for the American Journal of Ophthalmology, Ophthalmic Surgery and Lasers, and Journal of Glaucoma. She is a member of the Advisory Board for Review of Ophthalmology and Ocular Surgery News. Due to her clinical experience in ophthalmology, Dr. Moster has been involved with an array of educational programs and lectures affiliated with the Wills Eye Hospital, the American Glaucoma Society, and the American Academy of Ophthalmology, among others. The focus of her participation is diagnosis, therapy, and surgical intervention for glaucoma. Dr. Moster has received much recognition for her professional and philanthropic involvement in the field of ophthalmology, including an appreciation award from Eye Care America for her voluntary services in providing eye care to the public. In 1999, she was appointed Coordinator for “Glaucoma 2001” for the state of Pennsylvania, a public service project of the Foundation of the American Academy of Ophthalmology. Dr. Moster has been heralded by several publications including “Who’s Who” (Marquis Who’s Who LLC) and Philadelphia Magazine. She is a member of numerous medical and glaucoma related associations and has been listed in Best Doctors in America and “Top Doctors” in the Philadelphia area for many years. Dr. Moster has been invited to lecture nationally and internationally on surgical and medical treatment of glaucoma.
