Dr. Robert A. Kaufmann's response
I'm sorry to hear about your negative experience in our office, but appreciate your candid feedback on what we can do to improve our practice. Regarding Dr. Kaufmann's phone call to patients, Dr. K tries very hard to contact every patient in a timely manner after treatment cycles, both negative and positive. He calls at the end of clinic and his duties to his own family so sometimes those phone calls end up being later at night than either he or the patients would like. He also calls from his personal cell phone which, I'm sure you understand, is why his phone number is blocked. We strive to inform patients on all aspects of their treatment cycles, including if Dr. Kaufmann will not be in office at the time of an IUI procedure. Dr. Kaufmann would not have his nurses perform IUI's in his absence if he did not have the utmost confidence in their ability to complete the procedure properly. We do batch IVF cycles in our office, not because we do not have a full time embryologist on staff (we do), but to ensure Dr. Kaufmann is in office to perform retrievals and transfers himself. While it is clinically insignificant to have a full bladder for a trial transfer, Dr. Kaufmann does require a full bladder on the day of embryo transfer. Grading embryos and selecting embryos for transfer is very subjective unless genetic testing is done. Sometimes during a freeze/thaw, embryos may have been frozen at 4AA, but thaw and look closer to 4BB. Our embryologists are trained to select the best looking embryos with the highest chance for successful implantation. It is a policy of our office to give all patients a picture of the embryos they are going to have transferred. I hope this addressed the majority of your concerns. While we are sad your initial cycles did not work at our office, we are glad to hear the embryos created in our lab were successful and hope the best for you and your family. If you would like to discuss more in detail, please contact ivy@fwivf.com and I would be happy to talk with you.

Biography

Dr. Kaufmann is one of the top board-certified reproductive endocrinologist in Texas specializing in fertility care ranging from intrauterine insemination to in-vitro fertilization (IVF) and pre-implantation genetic diagnosis (PGD). Nationally recognized as a leader in IVF for high successful donor and third-party gestational surrogacy programs, Dr. Kaufmann continually invests considerable resources and his passion for this specialty into establishing the most comprehensive infertility program in the nation. Dr. Kaufmann changed the landscape of reproductive medicine with his research contributions and academic leadership. With over 20 years of experience, Dr. Kaufmann leads a recognized team of physicians, nurses, embryologists and support staff providing personalized patient care across the globe. Fort Worth Fertility demonstrates an extensive and proven track record with over 1,600 gestational carrier cycles and 2,500 PGD cases.
