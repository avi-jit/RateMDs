If you have a child who may require surgery, I encourage you to research the pediatric services offered by Kalispell Reg’l Medical Center. Upon my son’s initial Crohn’s diagnosis eight months ago, I began researching facilities in the Northwest and the US. As part of the due diligence, my son was seen by a director at another nationally recognized facility in anticipation that I may seek care outside the Flathead. I wouldn’t have hesitated to go outside the Flathead if I thought it was best for my son, as we do for my husband who has a chronic disease. Surprisingly, my due diligence revealed that many pediatric specialists at Kalispell Regional Medical Center have comparable education, training and experience as many of the pediatric specialists at the best children’s hospitals in the country. After thoughtful consideration, we determined it was best for my son if he had the surgery in Montana with Dr. Seifarth. In fact, if Dr. Seifarth did not practice in Kalispell, we may have flown to Cleveland Clinic (his previous location) to seek him out. While the entire pediatric team was quite impressive, I will speak specifically to Dr. Seifarth since this is a review for him. (I would be remiss not to at least mention his nurse practitioner, Rachel, who is a wonderful complement to Dr. Seifarth and is deserving of her own glowing review for many reasons.) Although I knew of Dr. Seifarth’s reputation, training and experience prior to my son’s appointment, the pre-operative appointment cemented the deal so to speak. Dr. Seifarth was the right surgeon for us. In the weeks after our first appointment with Dr. Seifarth, our confidence in our decision grew as we prepared for surgery. Looking back on the initial re-sectioning surgery and the weeks of Crohn’s-induced complications, including an emergency surgery and a month in the hospital, we firmly believe we could not have placed our son’s young life in the hands of a better surgeon. Over the course of a month under Dr. Seifarth’s daily care (as well as other staff on the team), I was able to observe Dr. Seifarth and the team under a variety of circumstances --- a major surgery that was supposed to include a fairly quick recovery, the realization that something was very wrong requiring an immediate follow-up surgery (ostomy), the insertion of four drains over the course of two weeks, struggles with my son’s reporting of pain, daily follow-up visits during rounds, etc. -- I observed Dr. Seifarth’s consistent demeanor in calm times and in stressful times. Dr. Seifarth is extremely competent (research his training and experience for yourself). He took time to explain things in a way that an inquisitive and capable mom would understand and then in a way that a 12-year-old boy would understand (he draws pictures and uses kid-friendly terms). He invited and answered questions. He was humble and at ease. I imagine anyone who spends a little time with him would think he was a good guy. He has a bedside manner that makes it apparent that pediatrics is his love. He developed a relationship with my son (not through me, but by talking directly to my son and respecting him as the patient). For example, I observed him joke often with my son, tell goofy stories, show him photos of his pets, and talk about his own children. He encouraged my son on the worst days and had fun with him on the best days. He treated my son as though my son was his most important patient. Never once in the month we were in the hospital did he seem rushed or like he didn’t have all the time in the world for my son. I am so grateful that KMRC has chosen to invest so much time, money, and energy into developing a pediatric unit that can service families in Montana without the need to go out-of-state. I am grateful that our beautiful surroundings (Glacier Park, Big Mountain, Flathead Lake, etc.) help entice highly-qualified doctors from all over the country to choose to practice here. I simply did not have an appreciation of what was happening in my own community until my family was thrust into the middle of this chronic disease. I encourage you to investigate the pediatric offerings at KMRC before going out of state.

Dr. Federico Seifarth recently performed major surgeries on my young relative including one emergency surgery. Prior to the first of these surgeries, the child’s well-educated and detail-oriented parents did much research including traveling to a nationally-known children’s hospital for consultation. After much vetting, Dr. Seifarth was chosen as the one to perform the surgery. Briefly, I am in a profession with much contact with medical professionals when the care provided does not meet appropriate standards or when bad outcomes occur even with the best of care. As such, I am familiar with attributes of the best and the worst physicians in various specialties. During my visits to Kalispell Regional Healthcare, I heard Dr. Seifarth’s communications with my young relative and with the child’s parents and observed how Dr. Seifarth interacted with all. Dr. Seifarth’s compassion and concern for his patient and the patient’s family were evident. Prior to an urgent surgery and in the face of many unknowns, Dr. Seifarth’s calming, confident, patient demeanor helped both the child and the parents. Dr. Seifarth handled the complex informed consent process in textbook fashion. He did not over-promise and under-deliver. Instead, he was upfront and explained the potential scenarios that would take place depending on what he found in the OR. He did this with the child and the parents separately with consents tailored for each. After hours of surgery, Dr. Seifarth explained in detail what he had seen and done in the operating room. He explained the potential short-term and long-term prognoses and also explained what the responses to the various scenarios would be. He happily answered many questions and requests for clarification from the parents. Upon request of the parents to involve a completely unknown medical specialist the family knew and trusted, Dr. Seifarth did so without hesitation. He willingly updated and involved that trusted infectious disease professional who was not even in the same geographic area. In short, Dr. Seifarth’s humility, knowledge and caring nature impressed even this family friend/medical specialist. My interactions and knowledge of Dr. Seifarth allow me to conclude that the man is not only a great surgeon but also a great human being. His humble manner puts everyone around him at ease as much as possible in the face of frightening situations. Most importantly, this surgeon showed genuine interest in the well-being of his young patient. I observed him taking time to explain the situation in a way a youngster could comprehend. He welcomed and answered all questions. He almost lingered in the room at times attempting to make my sad and scared relative smile. Finally, I also observed Dr. Seifarth during some interactions with staff. In each of these interactions, Dr. Seifarth treated others respect and kindness. In conclusion, despite reading Dr. Seifarth’s reviews, considering his training and experience, and hearing about Dr. Seifarth from my relatives, I reserved judgment until I felt I had enough information to reach my own conclusion. Simply put, I believe Dr. Seifarth’s clinical and surgical skills put him in an elite group of only a handful of the best pediatric surgeons in this country. Montana is fortunate to have him. I’ve never written an online review of anything or anyone until now but feel I need to make something akin to a public service announcement. If you are in the unfortunate position of needing to place your child’s life in the hands of a surgeon, please do your child and yourself a favor and research the doctors at Kalispell Regional Healthcare. I assure you this particular surgeon, Dr. Seifarth, will prove worthy of the trust and confidence placed in him.
