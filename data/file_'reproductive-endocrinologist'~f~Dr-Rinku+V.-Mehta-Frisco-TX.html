Biography

Dr. Mehta is the Medical Director of Frisco Institute for Reproductive Medicine. She is board certified in reproductive endocrinology and infertility and has helped hundreds of patients successfully start their families since 2003. Helping patients realize their dreams of building a family is a source of great fulfillment for her. Having made the fertility journey herself, Dr. Mehta understands the emotional rollercoaster and anxiety that dealing with infertility brings to many couples. She is committed to providing the most personal, individualized care and sees every patient at every visit, performing all sonograms and procedures herself. Dr. Mehta graduated Summa Cum Laude from the University of North Texas in Denton and then went on to medical school at the University of Texas in Galveston, where she graduated with honors and was selected for membership in the prestigious Alpha Omega Alpha Medical Honor Society. While in medical school, she won several awards, tutored fellow students in neuroscience, and was the president of Phi Rho Sigma Medical Fraternity. She also spent time volunteering at a local charity clinic. She then completed her Ob/Gyn residency at the University of Texas at Galveston followed by subspecialty fellowship training in reproductive endocrinology and infertility at the University of California San Diego, which is renowned for its research in reproductive biology. Dr. Mehta has actively pursued research in embryo development and polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) and published several articles in leading journals, as well as co-authoring a book chapter. Dr. Mehta has previously held academic appointments at UTMB Galveston and UT Southwestern Medical School. She serves as a member of the American Society for Reproductive Medicine, Society for Reproductive Endocrinology and Infertility, American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists, Texas Medical Association and the Alpha Omega Alpha Medical Society. Dr. Mehta’s areas of special interest include PCOS, obesity, fertility preservation, ovarian aging and hysteroscopic surgery.
