First let me give you a little background on myself: I am a double major in chemistry and medical ethics as well as a certified EMT. When I found out that my son would be born with a congenital heart defect (Tetralogy of Fallot), I went on a search for the best care available. All pediatric cardiac centers will put on a convincing pitch for your little one, but they are NOT all created equal. Living in Southwest Florida, I had four options within driving distance: Miami, Orlando, All Children’s in Tampa, and St. Joseph’s in Tampa. I also considered going all the way to Boston Children’s. To any parents looking for the best care for their child, I have done the leg work for you. Go to St. Joseph’s. It’s a new program and doesn’t have the notoriety (yet) that the other programs have, but what it does have is the NUMBERS. They have the best survival rates and least number of days for a hospital stay. This says way more about patient care than any glossy brochure. Don’t believe me? Demand the numbers from other hospitals. Look at the complexity of the procedure that your child will have (1-5, 5 being the most complex. Tetralogy of Fallot is a 3). Mortality rate tells you how many patients don’t survive. Patients who recover faster don’t spend as many days in the hospital. Also look at the nurse to child ratio. St. Joesph’s is not a teaching hospital. No one operating on or treating your child is learning, they are already the experts in their field. This is HUGE and should not be overlooked when considering one of the large centers, like Boston Children’s. Especially if you have textbook case, it is more likely that a resident will perform the surgery instead of an experienced attending. Now let me tell you about Dr. Langley. He is AMAZING. I have worked with many doctors throughout my career and hands down, Dr. Langley is one of the best. Besides being brilliant and extremely talented, Dr. Langley is kind and compassionate. I will never forget walking into my 3 week old son’s intensive care room and seeing Dr. Langely holding and talking to him, or the way he would rub my son’s head any time he listened to his heart. I don’t know if his English accent makes him sound more impressive, but it certainly doesn’t hurt. The nursing and hospital staff are well above average. Only the best nurses at the hospital are in the PCICU (pediatric cardiac intensive care unit) and must pass certain tests to be able to work there. They are knowledgeable and very careful about letting you care for your child and getting you involved. I always felt comfortable leaving my son with them while I went to eat or rest. Right before and well after surgery, my son had one nurse dedicated to his care. Only during the last two days, when he was stable and just being watched, did his nurse have one other patient to care for. Overall, I could not be happier with our entire experience at St. Joesph’s. From tours while I was still pregnant, delivering at the women’s hospital across the street (which felt more like a spa than a hospital), to the incredible cardiologists in the Pediatrix group, my son is a happy, healthy, and thriving heart surgery survivor because of my choice to bring him to Dr. Langely at St. Joesph’s. My son needs one more procedure in a few months and without any doubt we will be going back to Dr. Langley. I will not bring him to anyone else.

Biography

Dr. Stephen Langley is the Chief of Pediatric and Congenital Cardiac Surgery at St. Joseph's Children's Hospital in Tampa, FL and a clinical professor at the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center. He received his medical degree from London University and underwent cardiac surgical training in Cambridge and Southampton, UK. After undertaking pediatric cardiac surgical fellowship at Great Ormond St. Hospital and at Birmingham Children's Hospital, in 2002 he became an attending cardiac surgeon at Southampton University Hospital. He moved to Portland, OR in 2007 and became the Chief of Pediatric and Congenital Cardiac Surgery at Oregon Health and Science University. In 2015 he moved to Tampa, FL to take up his current position.
