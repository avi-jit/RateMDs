Biography

Mark Samaha, M.D. is a facial plastic surgeon who is dedicated to quality care in every facet of his practice. His priority is to ensure his patients’ vision for esthetic improvement becomes a reality. His approach emphasizes achieving a natural look by balancing facial proportions to create esthetic harmony. Dr. Samaha specializes in minimally invasive techniques. He performs only facial esthetic procedures, most commonly rhinoplasty, facelift, blepharoplasty (eyelid surgery), forehead lift (also called brow lift), chin augmentation, and otoplasty (ear reshaping). In addition, Dysport and skin fillers are a prominent part of his practice. After earning his medical degree from Queen’s University in Ontario, Canada, Dr. Samaha completed his residency training at McGill University. Following this, he decided to pursue extra-specialized training and completed a prestigious fellowship at Harvard Medical School’s Massachusetts Eye and Ear Infirmary. Dr. Samaha is double-board certified by both the American Board of Facial Plastic Surgery and the American Board of otolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery. He is also certified by the Royal College of Surgeons of Canada. He is a member of several scientific societies, including the American Academy of Facial Plastic and Reconstructive surgery and the American Academy of Otolaryngology - Head and Neck Surgery. He is a director of the Canadian Academy of Facial Plastic and Reconstructive surgery. Dr. Samaha has been practicing the art of facial plastic surgery since 2002. He constantly remains at the very cutting edge of the science and art of facial plastic surgery. As an associate professor at McGill University, Dr. Samaha regularly teaches medical students, residents, and other physicians. For his various teaching activities, Dr. Samaha has been honored with teaching awards over the years. He is on staff at the McGill University Health Centre and runs a private clinic where he performs his esthetic procedures.
